Aviture First 30

Built for On-boarding New Hires

By Brian Pope
Vision:

Create a better way for new employees to become acclimated into Aviture's culture, systems and initiatives.

Goal:

An interactive way to onboard new employees with current Aviture employees, benefits and culture.

Shared Purpose:

New employees begin a scavenger hunt during their first 30 days of starting. The purpose is to drive engagement with Aviture. The new hires focus is to complete as many tasks as they can within the given time limit. On the other side of the coin, Aviturians have a chance to meet and help acclimate new hires into Aviture’s ecosystem.>

Principles:

Interactive

The idea of this scavenger hunt is to pair learning with hands on activities. To get new hires moving around the office, meeting new people, and understanding who Aviture is.

Self Sustainable

This on-boarding system is flexible to match Aviture’s evolving culture and benefits. Tasks should be able to be added and removed when appropriate.

Accessible

The Aviture first 30 should be easy to access from a new hires perspective. Completion of tasks should be the challenge, not the sign off.

Fairness

All tasks must be available during the new hires first 30 days. Tasks that are seasonal will not be added. Tasks must fit Avitures current culture and will be curated to match over time.
